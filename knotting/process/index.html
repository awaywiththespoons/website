Knotting Processes

Knots resonated with some of the concepts of learning we have been exploring in Design Education. Initially, I was drawn to knots through Engeström’s theory of learning, in which he likens the learning process to knot-making. Juliet Sprake describes it here:

“Learning is exploratory movement in space and the meaningful encounters that occur between ‘heterogeneous actors and their lives of movement’ that Engeström likens to knot forming. These collaborative ‘knots’ are particularly critical between nonlinear wayfaring trails and predominantly straightforward lines of transport” (Sprake, 2012)

Knots remind me of embodied and dynamic learning –both are ways of being “situated” in learning. Being situated in learning is to learn through multiple sensory experiences, and learn through interconnecting perspectives. Knots are a metaphor for this, because of their affordance to make connections through their material properties.
