Space for knots

Knotting also led me to places I had not anticipated, such as baking a 'mug-cake' in a polythene bag! .

Baking a mug-cake in a bag refers to explorations in to the space that defines and controls knots. This is related to the Environment section. Space and Environment both explore the way that the local area to the knot has an impact in the way that it is formed. In Space however, I explore many more nuances of these 'local areas'.

Space started as a way to explore how knots are formed by other materials. I started this section through collaborating with molecular scientist Dave Glowacki. We got talking about knots and molecules, and from these conversations I learned that some theories suggest that DNA is coded through knots. Because there are few studies into knotting things so small, it is unclear how knotting on a molecular level happens, and we started to imagine how this would be possible. I made a series of knots in bags as a way to explore agency and knots. How do they form on their own? What are the conditions needed to form a knot?

Preparing to fill the bags

Bags full: I placed 23 pairs of strings in bags (the number is based on the number of human chromosones). Then filled the bags with: nothing (air), jelly, water, cake, resin, latex, angel delight, and seeds

I filmed the process of fill the bags (just for fun!)

To simulate movemnt, I made a 'bag' for the bags that could be worn around.
