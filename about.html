“It matters what matters we use to think other matters with; it matters what stories we tell to tell other stories with; it matters what knot knot knots, what thoughts think thoughts, what descriptions describe descriptions, what ties tie ties. It matters what stories make worlds, what worlds make stories”
Donna Haraway, Staying with the Trouble.

I am a designer-educator based in Bristol, UK. My work is based at the interesection of arts, technology and socially enagaged arts practices. I specialise in re-desigining, re-imagining, and making DIY sensors.

I work in public spaces, often with third sector partners, and in the past few years I have been resident in a number of museums and arts spaces including Knowle West Media Centre in Bristol, Exploratorium in San Francisco, Somerset House in London, and Wired Lab in Tokyo. I co-run E-stitches Bristol, a monthly e-textile meetup based at the Pervasive Media Studio.

In 2018 I started an AHRC funded PhD at Goldsmiths, University of London, where I am exploring learning and sensing practices.
