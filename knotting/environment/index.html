Environment

The knot experiments I made in the Environment section were used as a metaphor for situated learning. I was interested in situated knotting. How is knotting situated? How does the situation impact the knotwork? I made a series of experiments where I left strings to knot over a time period in different contexts. The knot strands (above) were left in different environments for 5 days.

The result was a series of beautifully interconnected knotworks. The lines of strings that formed the knots moved about the environment without any regard for directionality. This resonates with how Engeström views learning:

“We habitually tend to depict learning and development as vertical processes, aimed at elevating humans upward, to higher levels of competence...I suggest that we construct a complementary perspective, namely that of horizontal or sideways learning and development.” (Engeström, 2004)

Engeström reflects on the negative associations with upwardly linier metaphors for learning. The metaphor he prefers to use for learning is multi-directional, like the lines of strings when they form a knot in the hangings.
